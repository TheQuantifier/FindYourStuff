Why it matters

Most people do not lose things. They lose context.

Find Your Stuff is built for the small failures that waste time: the battery that moved last weekend, the document tucked into the wrong drawer, the tool returned to a shelf that made sense at the time. A searchable memory reduces that friction and gives every item a traceable place.

  • Lower mental overheadYou do not have to remember every move yourself.
  • Shared clarityAnyone using the same account can see where something was last placed.
  • Better routinesThe act of saving a location makes storage habits more consistent.

Planned direction

From chat memory to a visual map of your spaces.

The long-term goal is not just to answer questions in chat. The app is being shaped to show rooms, storage areas, and exact placements visually, so you can browse a map of your home, office, or workshop and see where items belong.

  • Visual location mappingSee item placement in a more spatial way instead of only reading text.
  • Location categoriesCreate pages for rooms, closets, bins, shelves, and drawers.
  • Exact placement detailTrack both the broader location and the specific spot inside it.

Locations page vision

A structured inventory view alongside the conversational one.

The future locations page is meant to organize storage into categories that are easy to scan: for example, Garage, Hall Closet, Kitchen Pantry, or Office. Inside each location, items can be grouped by exact place such as top shelf, left drawer, back bin, or filing cabinet middle folder. Chat remains the fastest input method, while the locations page becomes the clearest browsing method.
